## 1. Brand Identity Overview

### Brand Essence

Golden Stone QLD exists at the intersection of skilled trade and lasting quality. We cut, finish, and install stone benchtops that people will live with for decades — and we treat every job with that weight in mind. Not luxury for its own sake. Not speed at the cost of quality. Just professional stonemasonry, done properly.

**Three words that anchor every brand decision:** craftsmanship, permanence, reliability.

### Tagline

> **Crafted in Stone. Built to Last.**

Works across both audiences:
- For joineries: a promise of trade quality and reliability
- For home owners: the permanence of stone as a material and a decision

### Mission Statement

> Founded on craftsmanship and built on a dream — bringing premium stonemasonry to homes across South East Queensland.

---

## 2. Brand Story

Golden Stone QLD was founded by partners pursuing something they could truly call their own — a business built on real skill, not just ambition. One co-founder brings over 15 years of stonemasonry experience across New Zealand and Australia, with hands-on knowledge of every part of the process: templating, cutting, finishing, installation.

The name carries more than a surface meaning.

**Gold** represents royalty, lasting value, and quality that doesn't compromise. It's the colour of things worth keeping.

**Gold is also element 79 on the periodic table** — chemical symbol **Au**. And **Au** are the first two letters of **Australia** — the country we proudly call home, and the market we serve.

So Golden Stone isn't a metaphor. It's a statement: Australian-made quality, backed by real experience, built to last.

### Concept D — Isometric Stone Slab ✅ OFFICIAL DIRECTION

An isometric slab mark — three visible faces of a stone benchtop in perspective:
- **Top face (Brass Gold `#B8922A`):** the finished working surface, the part the client sees
- **Front face (Deep Black `#111111`):** the stone body and thickness
- **Right edge (Dark Gold `#7A5F1A`):** depth and shadow

**Why Concept D won:**

1. **Industry-specific without being literal** — it reads as a stone slab immediately, without using a trowel, hammer, or any clichéd trade symbol
2. **The metaphor is exact** — the gold IS the surface (the finished benchtop); the black IS the stone foundation. The mark is the product
3. **Dimensional and architectural** — the isometric perspective gives weight and structure that flat marks lack
4. **Works at all scales** — strong as a favicon, powerful on a van wrap, refined on a document
5. **Palette-native** — gold and black are the v2 palette; the mark cannot be confused for another brand's colours

Files: `concept-d-wordmark.svg` ← primary use, `concept-d-mono.svg`, `concept-d-inverted.svg`

### Logo Usage Rules

**Clear Space**
Maintain clear space equal to the height of the slab mark on all sides.

**Minimum Digital Size**
- Full logo (mark + wordmark): 200px wide minimum
- Mark only: 32px minimum

**Approved Backgrounds**

| Background | Variant |
|------------|---------|
| Warm Cream `#F5F0E8` | Full colour (`concept-d-wordmark.svg`) |
| White | Full colour |
| Deep Black `#111111` | Inverted (`concept-d-inverted.svg`) |
| Dark photography | Inverted |
| Single-colour print | Mono (`concept-d-mono.svg`) |

**Logo Don'ts**
- ❌ Do not recolour the slab faces outside the approved palette
- ❌ Do not flatten the mark to 2D (removes the dimensionality that makes it work)
- ❌ Do not add drop shadows, gradients, glows, or shine effects
- ❌ Do not stretch or distort proportions
- ❌ Do not rotate the mark
- ❌ Do not place on busy photographic backgrounds without a solid backdrop
- ❌ Do not recreate in Word or PowerPoint — use the SVG files

## 7. Voice & Tone

### Brand Personality

```
Formal ←─────────────────●────→ Casual       (professional, not stiff)
Simple ←●──────────────────────→ Complex      (plain Australian English)
Serious ←────────●─────────────→ Playful      (confident, not humourless)
Reserved ←────●────────────────→ Expressive   (assured, not loud)
```

### Writing Standards

- **Australian English spelling** throughout: colour, realise, favourite, organisation, centre, metre
- Sentence case for body copy; uppercase allowed for display headings and labels only
- No exclamation marks in professional contexts
- Numbers: spell out one to nine; numerals for 10+; always numerals for measurements and prices

### Voice Principles

---

**1 — Be direct. Say what you mean.**

> **Say this:** "We'll measure up, quote within 24 hours, and lock in your install date — usually within 2 weeks."
> **Not this:** "Our bespoke consultation process ensures a tailored journey from concept through to completion."

**B2B:** "Turnaround is typically 5–7 business days from template. We'll confirm your slot when the order is placed."
**B2C:** "Get a quote by tomorrow. Installation booked within the week for most jobs."

---

**2 — Let experience show through knowledge, not claims.**

> **Say this:** "Not sure whether to go with marble or quartzite? We'll walk you through the real trade-offs for your kitchen."
> **Not this:** "As industry leaders in premium stonemasonry, our unparalleled expertise sets us apart."

**B2B:** "We've run high-volume joinery programs before. Send us your specs and we'll price it properly."
**B2C:** "We've installed hundreds of kitchens across South East Queensland. We'll tell you what works."

---

**3 — Price confidence, not apology.**

> **Say this:** "Good stonework doesn't have to cost a fortune. Most standard kitchens come in well under what people expect."
> **Not this:** "We offer cost-effective solutions without compromising on quality."

**B2B:** "Our pricing is structured for volume joinery work. The more consistent the workflow, the better the rate."
**B2C:** "Get a free quote — no obligation, no pressure."

---

**4 — Compliance is a fact, not a campaign.**

> **Say this:** "We only supply silica-free engineered stone — safer for tradespeople, and compliant with Australian law since July 2024."
> **Not this:** "We are proud to be at the forefront of the silica-safe movement in the Australian stone industry."

---

**5 — Queensland, not the world.**

> **Say this:** "We cover Brisbane, Ipswich, Logan, Gold Coast, and surrounding South East Queensland — no surcharges within our service area."
> **Not this:** "We service all areas."

---

### Prohibited Terms

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| bespoke | Signals luxury boutique — wrong register |
| exclusive | Same |
| artisanal craftsmanship | Rustic cliché |
| timeless | Overused, vague |
| heritage craft | Wrong tone |
| unparalleled / industry-leading | Hollow superlative |
| journey | Marketing filler |
| passionate about stone | Every trade business says this |
| we strive to | Weak — just say what you do |
| cost-effective solutions | Corporate non-speak |

## 12. Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] Logo is the correct variant for the background (colour / mono / inverted)
- [ ] Clear space respected around the mark
- [ ] All colours from v2 palette — no v1 navy/steel blue values
- [ ] Fonts: Barlow Semi Condensed (headings), DM Sans (body)
- [ ] Australian English spelling throughout
- [ ] Copy checked against Voice Principles — no prohibited terms
- [ ] Photography: accurate-to-life, no over-styled editorial shots
- [ ] Location: only claim service areas listed in Section 9
- [ ] Silica-free mention: only when referring to engineered stone
- [ ] WCAG AA contrast verified for all text elements
